 Arizona is definitely a key state to watch this presidential election cycle. It's a border state where a few years ago lawmakers approved one of the nation's toughest anti-immigration laws. Traditionally, Arizona has also been a red state, voting Republican in most recent presidential contests. But that might be shifting this year. The conservative Arizona Republic newspaper this week endorsed Hillary Clinton for president, the first time it's endorsed a Democrat over a Republican in its entire 126-year history.
